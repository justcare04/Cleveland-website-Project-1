About Cleveland

Cleveland, situated on the southern shore of Lake Erie, is a vibrant city known for its rich cultural heritage, world-class museums, and passionate sports fans. Home to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, Cleveland has earned its nickname as "The Rock and Roll Capital of the World."

Quick Facts

  • Founded: 1796
  • Population: Approximately 372,624 (2020)
  • Location: Northeast Ohio, on Lake Erie
  • Known for: Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, Cleveland Clinic, Cultural Gardens

Popular Attractions

Cleveland Museum of Art

One of the most respected art museums in the world, with free admission. Beautiful grounds feature a reflecting pool and classical architecture.

Rock and Roll Hall of Fame

Explore the history of rock and roll in this iconic museum, featuring a stunning modern architectural design on the shores of Lake Erie.

West Side Market

Historic public market featuring local vendors and ethnic foods in a beautiful brick building dating back to 1912.
